Topology-Driven TVS Reliability & Cross-Domain Latch-Up Prevention

Integrating Calibre PERC LDL Structural Verification with Experimental TVS Characterization

1. Technical Background

In advanced mixed-signal SoCs, high-voltage TVS protection structures coexist with low-voltage core devices. While TVS ensures strong ESD discharge capability, its low-resistance clamp path may unintentionally reduce effective well resistance and increase parasitic SCR susceptibility.

Strong clamp path + Low well resistance + Cross-domain adjacency = Latch-up risk amplifier
---

2. Failure Mechanism: TVS + Cross-Domain Interaction

The parasitic PNPN structure in CMOS naturally forms an SCR. Improper domain isolation and well sharing can lower the holding current threshold.

  • Shared N-well across domains
  • Insufficient guard ring insertion
  • Low bulk resistance near large TVS diffusion
  • Domain bias ramp triggering parasitic path
---

3. Structural Verification Using PERC LDL

Topology-Based Checks

  • IO → Clamp → GND path validation
  • Cross-domain well adjacency detection
  • Guard ring completeness verification

Resistance-Based Qualification

  • P2P well-to-well resistance extraction
  • Bulk resistance imbalance detection
  • Domain-aware threshold criteria
LDL prevents structural latch-up candidates before silicon validation.
---

4. Experimental TVS Characterization Methodology

4.1 DC Characterization

  • Semiconductor Parameter Analyzer (Keysight B1500 / Keithley 4200A)
  • Extract: Vbr, leakage current, holding voltage

4.2 TLP (Transmission Line Pulse)

  • Pulse width ~100ns
  • Extract: Trigger voltage (Vt1), Dynamic resistance (Rd), Failure current (It2)

4.3 VF-TLP (Sub-ns Pulse)

  • CDM-like stress evaluation
  • Clamp overshoot measurement

4.4 Latch-Up Susceptibility Test

  • Domain bias ramp test
  • Well bias injection
  • Substrate current monitoring
---

5. Laboratory Measurement Setup

The DUT (Device Under Test) is characterized under DC, pulsed, and transient stress while monitoring clamp voltage, substrate current, and holding behavior.

7. Correlation: Structural Verification vs Measurement

LDL structural screening identified cross-domain low-resistance paths prior to silicon. TLP characterization confirmed reduction in snapback imbalance and increased holding current margin.

Structural verification (LDL) + Physical validation (Lab measurement) = Robust TVS qualification flow
---

8. Technical Takeaways

  • TVS clamp strength must be balanced with domain isolation
  • Well resistance is a key latch-up control parameter
  • Cross-domain adjacency must be topology-verified
  • Measurement correlation is essential for model calibration

Traditional DRC verifies geometry. LDL verifies robustness. Laboratory validation confirms physics.
