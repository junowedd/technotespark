EUV vs ArF Immersion Lithography

Introduction

In semiconductor manufacturing, lithography is used to transfer circuit patterns onto silicon wafers. The wavelength of light used in lithography determines the resolution of patterns that can be achieved. EUV and ArF immersion lithography are two advanced technologies used for different process nodes.

Comparison Diagram

Key Differences

  • EUV Resist: Uses 13.5 nm wavelength light. High energy photons require high sensitivity resist. Suitable for sub-5nm nodes. Process occurs in vacuum.
  • ArF Immersion Resist: Uses 193 nm wavelength light. Water immersion improves resolution. Focuses on chemical stability and process compatibility for 7-10nm nodes.

Compatibility Meaning

When a material or process is compatible with both EUV and ArF immersion lithography, it means the resist, mask, or equipment can function in both lithography processes while maintaining performance and pattern quality. This provides process flexibility and reduces development costs.
